Explication is the replacement of an extant, inexact or ambiguous concept with
a new, more precise or useful concept [^1].

Explication is a normative act, in that it applies value judgements about what properties a concept _should_ have. This may
involve a significant transformation of the original concept.

Philosophers have suggested criteria for a successful explication,
including that the new concept is more clear and/or useful, but also that the new
concept can be substituted for the old concept without a substantial loss of meaning.

[^1]: In the lingo of analytic philosophy, an _explicandum_ is replaced by an _explicatum_.
